Sonorous Brushstrokes

A Cross-Modal AI Framework for Translating Erik Satie's Fin-de-Siècle Parisian Solitude into Synesthetic Visual Art

Eun Ji Park · Seoul National University · 2026

Abstract Case Studies Method Code About

Abstract

This article proposes a cross-modal AI framework that translates piano works by Erik Satie into period-faithful visual artworks. The framework anchors its interpretive choices in Satie's biographical solitude, twenty-seven years of monastic life in Arcueil, his only documented love affair with Suzanne Valadon in 1893, the unsent letters and hidden compositions discovered after his death, treating this biographical anchor as a curatorial input alongside the audio. The architecture integrates contrastive audio-language embeddings, low-level acoustic descriptors, and Stable Diffusion XL with IP-Adapter conditioned through curated fin-de-siècle Parisian visual references. Three case studies cover Gymnopédie No. 1, Gnossienne No. 1, and Vexations, spanning the affair's emotional arc. The article frames AI as interpretive collaborator rather than substitutive imitator.

Three Case Studies

Each piece is shown alongside its biographical anchor, the prompt that drove generation, the IP-Adapter reference set, and an authorial reading of the resulting image.

The piece precedes the Valadon affair by five years, in the Auberge du Clou years, when Satie was already living the eccentric ritual life but had not yet experienced the brief disruption of returned attention that 1893 would bring. The lone walker's bowed head and contemplative gait read as an emblem of pre-affair loneliness, the Auberge du Clou years compressed into a single moment of solitary motion five years before the brief 1893 disruption.

Composed in the year between the first Gymnopédies and the Rose+Croix commissions, a period of intensified solitude during which Satie was developing the harmonic vocabulary that Valadon would, three years later, identify as the voice she had been listening to. The visual anchor was deliberately shifted away from the Orientalist framing of the v3 manuscript: on inspection of how IP-Adapter image generation imports Orientalist tropes, "imagined East" was rephrased as "modal solitude, contemplation before love."

Visual subject

Manner of

0.30 (low, preserve "no people" prompt)
Seed

The score is a single page of fewer than two minutes, accompanied by Satie's instruction to repeat it 840 times in the deepest silence and serious immobilities. The work was hidden in his Arcueil room for thirty-two years; it was discovered, alongside the unsent Bonjour Biqui song and the bundle of unsent letters to Valadon, only in 1925 after his death. The pipeline answers the music's temporal repetition (840×) with an analogous spatial repetition (the boiserie corridor receding to a vanishing point), substituting space for the time the music asks for. We treat Vexations not as a single still but as the paradigmatic still: the corridor is one frame of a sequence that, in principle, could extend through twenty hours.

Method Highlights

Biographical anchoring

Each piece carries a piece-specific editorial phrase that names the moment of Satie's life from which it emerged. These phrases are released with the source code so that other research groups can revise, contest, or replace them by editing a single dictionary in src/prompts.py.

Per-piece IP-Adapter scale

The IP-Adapter scale is set per piece between 0.30 and 0.40 in the v1.0.1 release: 0.35 for Gymnopédie (Symbolist landscape with restrained reference influence), 0.40 for Gnossienne (single-figure interior with restrained Puvis + Redon), and 0.30 for Vexations (the prompt's empty corridor must survive the visual conditioning).

Lexical bridge

A handcrafted dictionary translates audio descriptors into image-prompt vocabulary. Four perceptual dimensions (palette, rhythm, surface, mood) × three percentile bands = twelve canonical phrases. Auditable: an expert can predict what phrase any given audio profile produces.

CLIP token discipline

SDXL's CLIP encoder truncates at 77 tokens. All four prompt components (subject, biographical, period, mood) are aggressively compressed to fit. Earlier longer formulations were silently truncated, with the result that biographical material was lost from the prompt.

What the Case Studies Show

  1. The IP-Adapter reference set is the dominant source of stylistic legibility. When the prompt asks for a Khnopff/Delville scene but the reference set is Puvis-leaning, the image still resolves toward Puvis. The per-piece scale is therefore the principal lever balancing visual style grounding against prompt fidelity.
  2. Biographical anchoring measurably alters the image's interpretive valence. The Gnossienne image, which under the v3 anchor's "imagined East" framing tended to import Orientalist tropes, reorganises around contemplative pre-romantic solitude when the anchor is rephrased as "modal solitude, contemplation before love", a swap that costs four tokens and changes the Salonist legibility entirely.
  3. The system handles temporally constituted works (Vexations) by displacement rather than by representation. A piece whose meaning is constituted by 840 repetitions over twenty hours cannot be illustrated in a single still; the pipeline substitutes an analogous spatial repetition (boiserie corridor receding to a vanishing point) for the temporal repetition the music asks for.
